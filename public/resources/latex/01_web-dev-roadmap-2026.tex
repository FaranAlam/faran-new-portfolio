\begin{infobox}{How to Build These Projects Like a Professional}
For every portfolio project, follow this structure:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=4pt]
    \item[\ding{52}] \textbf{Planning:} Define problem, target user, and 5--8 must-have features.

This one habit separates tutorial followers from job-ready developers.

\section{Beginner Action Plan (First 90 Days)}

\begin{newbox}
If you are a complete beginner, do not try to learn everything at once. Focus on \textbf{consistency over intensity}. Study 2--3 hours daily, build mini projects weekly, and revise every Sunday.
\end{newbox}

\subsection{Daily Study Structure (2.5 Hours)}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|>{\bfseries\color{FaranPurple}}l|X|>{\centering\arraybackslash}p{2.8cm}|}
\hline
\rowcolor{FaranDark}
\color{white}\textbf{Block} & \color{white}\textbf{What to Do} & \color{white}\textbf{Duration} \\
\hline
Concept Learning & Watch docs/video and take handwritten notes & 45 min \\
\hline
\rowcolor{FaranLight}
Guided Practice & Code along with 1 focused tutorial & 45 min \\
\hline
Independent Build & Build same idea without looking at tutorial & 45 min \\
\hline
\rowcolor{FaranLight}
Revision & Recap mistakes and update your personal notes & 15 min \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

\subsection{Weekly Output Goal}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=5pt]
    \item[\ding{52}] 1 mini project pushed to GitHub with clean commit history
    \item[\ding{52}] 1 LinkedIn post showing what you built and what you learned
    \item[\ding{52}] 1 code review session (self-review or mentor-review)
    \item[\ding{52}] 1 topic revision sheet (JavaScript, React, or CSS)
\end{itemize}

\begin{tipbox}
\textbf{Beginner Rule:} If a topic takes too long, reduce scope but do not stop. Example: if full auth is hard, build only register/login UI first, then add backend next week.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Common Beginner Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)}
